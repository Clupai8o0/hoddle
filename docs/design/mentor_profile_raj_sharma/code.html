My Story

Where I started

Arriving in Melbourne from the bustling streets of Delhi was a sensory overload. While the coffee was better than I imagined, the academic culture at Monash felt like a different language altogether. My first semester was a wake-up call—I nearly failed my first major assignment because I didn't understand the nuances of Australian critical analysis.

"The biggest barrier isn't the language; it's the unspoken cultural expectations of the Australian classroom."

What I figured out

I realized that success in Melbourne requires a blend of resilience and 'unlearning.' I started visiting the learning lounges every single day, asking the "silly" questions, and reaching out to seniors who had walked this path. By year two, I wasn't just surviving; I was topping my finance units and leading the International Student Association.

What I wish I knew

Networking isn't about being transactional; it's about genuine curiosity. I wish I knew that your first internship in Melbourne is often found in a coffee shop conversation, not just a LinkedIn portal. I’m here to make sure you don’t spend three semesters figuring that out like I did.
